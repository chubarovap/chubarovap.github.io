Brief interpretation

In my data analysis, I went back to using the penguins data and looked at the correlation between body mass and flipper length. The scatter plot regression indicates a high positive correlation between the two variables, as flipper length increases, so does the body mass of the penguins. Given that the regression line had a close fit to all the scatter points, it shows that using flipper length of penguins can be a good indicator for how much their body mass would be as well. The correlation heatmap proves that there is a strong positive correlation between body mass and flipper length, and body mass and flipper length also have a strong correlation to bill length. However, when looking at the residual plot, it showed deviation from a simple linear relationship. This indicates that the regression could have over- or underpredicted the positive correlation between body mass and flipper length when looking at outliers. Yet, the deviation is not drastic, therefore showcasing that there is some non-linearity in the correlation; however, it is still expected that penguins with longer flipper length will have a higher body mass, but it can also depend on the species, since I did not control for it. The visualizations helped confirm my assumption that, due to body composition and body proportions, healthy penguins would have a high correlation between their flipper length and body mass. The graphs allowed for patterns to become clearer at first glance, as the scatterplot regression and heatmap demonstrated a positive correlation. The residual plot showed non-linear patterns in this data.
